Design & Build — one team, one contract, one accountable partner.

SQA's Design & Build capability is the core of what we do. We take projects from concept and feasibility through architectural design, engineering coordination, construction, and handover — under a single integrated contract, delivered with Japanese project management discipline.

Our Core Capability

Why we lead with Design & Build.

Traditional contracting separates design from construction — creating interfaces, coordination risk, and finger-pointing when problems arise. SQA's Design & Build model removes those interfaces entirely. Our in-house design, engineering, and construction teams plan and deliver your project together, from day one.

For clients, that means one point of responsibility, one commercial structure, and a single accountable team that sees your project from blueprint to keys. For complex industrial, commercial, and institutional projects — this is how SQA consistently delivers with speed, quality, and assurance.

Design & Build Services

Six integrated capabilities, one accountable team.

Every SQA Design & Build engagement draws on the six capabilities below — delivered by one coordinated team, not by separate firms stitched together under a management fee.

01  Concept & Feasibility

Highest-and-best-use analysis, massing studies, development yield, and indicative cost plans — giving clients the commercial picture before a single drawing is fixed.

Full construction delivery under SQA's G7 licence — with the same safety culture, subcontractor discipline, and reporting we apply to every project.

Technical Depth

IBS — the technical backbone of our Design & Build projects.

Industrialised Building Systems (IBS) are central to how SQA delivers Design & Build — particularly for industrial warehouses, commercial buildings, and large-span facilities. By prefabricating components under factory-controlled conditions and assembling them on site, we hit programme targets that conventional construction cannot reach.

Reduced Labour Risk

Prefabricated components reduce on-site labour intensity — mitigating exposure to labour shortages and variability in workmanship.

Reduced Wastage

Factory production minimises construction waste — better for cost, better for sustainability, and better for site tidiness.

Better Quality Control

Factory-made components deliver more consistent dimensional accuracy, finishes, and performance than in-situ alternatives.
