The people behind every SQA project.

Our culture combines Japanese work discipline with Malaysian team spirit. The result is a workforce that takes pride in delivery — on site, in the office, and across every function that supports a project.

Our People

Construction is ultimately a people business. Our engineers, project managers, site supervisors, commercial team, and support staff are the reason SQA delivers reliably for clients across industrial, commercial, institutional, and residential sectors.

We invest in our people for the long term — through structured onboarding, mentorship from senior leaders, on-the-job exposure across project types, and continuous learning. We believe strongly that competent, motivated teams are the most durable competitive advantage a contractor can build.

Life at SQA

Our culture, our community, our people.

Beyond the projects, SQA is a team that invests in its people — through team gatherings, community sponsorships, safety culture, and continuous learning.

What Defines Our Culture

Japanese discipline. Malaysian team spirit.

Our culture is shaped by the things our people consistently bring to work — across nationalities, departments, and project sites.

Teamwork

Site, design, and commercial teams working as one — without silos or hand-off gaps.

Discipline

Punctual reporting, structured methods, and accountability — drawn from our Japanese heritage.

Pride

Genuine ownership of project outcomes and the buildings we hand over to clients.

Diversity

A broad mix of backgrounds, experience levels, and perspectives across our offices and sites.

Growth

Opportunities to learn, take on responsibility, and progress within a growing contractor.

Talent Development

Growing the next generation of builders.

SQA invests in people for the long term. We support our team through structured onboarding, mentorship from senior engineers and project managers, and clear pathways for career progression.

  • Structured onboarding and progressive job exposure
  • Regular toolbox and safety briefings for all site personnel
  • Professional development support and certification sponsorship
  • Mentorship from experienced project managers and directors
  • Clear internal progression routes across commercial, engineering, and operations
