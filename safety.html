Everyone safe, every day.

Safety is the foundation of Assurance. A safe project is a successful project — and every injury is preventable.

“Safe projects are successful projects. All injuries are preventable.”

This principle sits at the centre of how SQA operates. Every site we run — regardless of size, sector, or client — is built on this commitment.

Safety as Culture

Discipline, not paperwork.

At SQA, safety is not a binder of rules on a shelf. It is part of how our people work, communicate, and hold each other accountable — every hour of every shift.

01

Methodology & Hazard Prevention

Every task is planned with its hazards identified and mitigated in advance. Method statements are prepared, reviewed, and followed.

02

Weekly Safety Appraisal Boards

Structured weekly reporting of site safety performance — reviewed by site leadership and escalated where required.

03

Workforce & Subcontractor Compliance

Clear safety expectations for all workers on site, including subcontractors — enforced by supervision and spot checks.

04

Daily Morning Exercise

Structured warm-up exercises before shift start — reducing physical injury risk and building team discipline.

05

Daily Toolbox Meetings

Daily briefings where the day's tasks, hazards, and coordination points are discussed with every team before work begins.

06

Continuous Training

Regular refresher training, competency checks, and certification renewal for all site personnel.

The Assurance Connection

Why safety is part of Assurance.

Our brand commitment — Speed, Quality, and Assurance — is anchored in how we manage risk. Speed without safety is reckless. Quality without safety is hollow. Assurance is what gives clients confidence that SQA's delivery is not just fast and well-built, but reliable and without compromise.

  • Safety as a non-negotiable component of every contract
  • Leadership accountability — all the way to the top
  • Transparent reporting to clients on safety performance
  • Investment in PPE, site welfare, and safe working environments
